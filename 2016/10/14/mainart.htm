Panicked by Growing Line at Omelette Bar, Student Chooses Salmonella Rather than Confrontation

By THE EDITORIAL BOARD

October 14th, 2016

IRWIN - Local Jonathan Ge ‘18 has contracted a mild strain of salmonella after consuming undercooked dairy products from the omelette bar last week.

He is currently being treated at the Al Rashid Health and Wellness Center.

Dickinson resident Jonathan Ge of room 109, who agreed to speak with Lawrenceville Out Loud on the condition of anonymity, said that he had woken up 3 hours early to prepare his omelette that day in order to avoid traffic and TSA security lines.

“I had woken up 3 hours early to prepare my omelette,” Ge recalls.

Prepared to cook a hearty sausage, spinach, tomato, and cheese omelette, a worried Ge observed the one person queue double in size.

“By this point, things were getting pretty bad. I decided to scrap the sausage, spinach, tomato, and cheese… [and] just go with the egg.”

As the IVth former poured oil onto his pan, the line now grew to three people. Ge awkwardly shifted his pan for three minutes, trying to determine whether the stove was actually on.

The ceramic Swiss-made pans used for the omelette bar are specially designed to lightly burn and ruin the outside of the omelette while leaving the inside watery and entirely uncooked. As Ge nervously eyed the now five person line, with each waiting person staring blankly, he decided that it was necessary to abandon his hopes of producing a cohesive, edible mass of food. Prodding the omelette with his spatula as one would poke a wet piece of paper with a stick, Ge decided that scrambled eggs would be his safest option, non-metaphorically, at this point given his limited time.

Shortly after scrambling, the next-in-line asked Ge, “Hey, I don’t want to be rude, but do you know when you-”

Panicked, Ge immediately proceeded to pour the semi-solid contents of his omelette onto his plate and scrape off the burned egg off his pan. After multiple attempts using the German-engineered soft, dull rubber spatula to clean the pan, Ge abandoned his dreams of handing a clean pan to a fellow Lawrentian.

With only -2 minutes until classes would start, Ge hastily ate/drank the contents of his plate. It was only until after classes when Ge began to feel ill. It would be another day until the initial onset of symptoms developed into salmonella.

Lawrenceville Out Loud recommends that all Lawrentians cook their omelettes until no liquid is found and only the regret of not simply getting a bagel remains.

Eric Cheng contributed reporting.
