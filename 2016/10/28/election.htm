Lawrenceville Out Loud Announces Endorsement in Tumultuous Presidential Election

By MADELEINE REINHARD ‘19

October 28th, 2016

LAWRENCEVILLE - The 2016 presidential election is arguably the most egregious yet; no one has seen anything like it. With candidates so widely disliked, voters will now even-have the option to vote for “no one,” the seeming preference of most people.

Disagreement between candidates about topics ranging from the treatment of immigrants to running the military is a dream-come-true for every comedy show. With a variety of ethnic and activist groups heavily offended, deciding which candidate to vote for has become a heavy, and largely private obligation. With the nation so fiercely divided, the result of the election will undoubtedly be a controversial one.

Nonetheless, the world’s attention will be drawn to the results of the 2016 Bulgarian presidential election, as Tsetska Tsacheva faces off against Rumen Radav and Трайчо Трайков and Ивайло Георгиев Калфин and Tatyana Doncheva and Plamen Oresharski and George Ganchev and Velizar Enchev and Veselin Mareshki and Dimitar Marinov.

Lawrenceville Out Loud doesn't actually endorse any candidate whatsoever, contrary to the message implied in this article or its title.

Madeleine Reinhard contributed reporting.
