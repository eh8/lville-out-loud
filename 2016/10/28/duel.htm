Lawrenceville Celebrates 125th Anniversary of House Flintlock Pistol Duelling

By THE EDITORIAL BOARD

October 28th, 2016

LAWRENCEVILLE - This week’s Wednesday marks the quasquicentennial of the School’s esteemed duelling program. Started in 1891 between Hamill and Davis House, pistol duelling has defined Lawrenceville’s House system and inter-schooling bonding.

For reasons unknown, the flintlock pistol duelling program has lost much of its participants as the 21th century emerged. In 2013, the Lawrenceville introduced House jousting as a safer and more inviting alternative to the century old tradition. After much controversy and rioting, flintlock pistol duelling returned, albeit in a modified 8 man tackle format. Despite much investment, duelling has mysteriously lost its former glory.

Boys Varsity Ricketyfinkelgollywockball Coach and Potions Master Sir Thomas P. Hatt ‘24 H’37 ‘43 ‘47 ‘53 ‘59 ‘-61 ‘67i ‘714 ‘73 ‘79 ‘83 ‘89 ‘493 P’44 ‘52 ‘65 ‘65 said that the dim future of flintlock pistol duelling “has largely lost appeal among today’s students for whatever reasons. Kids these days only want to be recruited to college and live beyond the age of 40.”

In centuries past, Hatt recalls that dozens of eager students would participate in the program, but now each House tends to have teams that “are at best 8-10, with realistically only 6 at any given moment due to minor injuries or deaths.”

Despite lacking participation, House duelling has had a vibrant history. The 4th oldest preparatory school duelling league has three distinguished awards between three pairs of Circle Houses. For instance, Kennedy and Hamill have competed for 17 Chuck E. Cheese's™ tickets for several decades.

“I remember the legendary 1923 Kennedy-Hamill duel… it was truly spectacular and went on for a while. We were all dead by the end of the day… Just to clarify I meant dead in a metaphorical sense. We were all perfectly fine,” said one alumni whose name would take too long to fabricate.

However faint the future of duelling may be, the cherished memories it has brought, and continues to bring, to a weary student body will certainty not be easily forgotten.

“House flintlock pistol duelling embodies the spirit of the House. If not House flintlock pistol duelling,” says the alumni, “I’m confident that something else, perhaps even better, will take its place in history.”

Eric Cheng contributed reporting.
